# TDA00 Relationships And Tone

Auxiliary only. JP wording remains primary.

## Will and Lilia

- Start with rescue and professional distance.
- Tension develops around whether searching for other survivors is possible.
- Lilia pushes from duty and survivor guilt; Will pushes back from operational limits and withheld classified information.
- Their conflict is not simple hostility. It mixes guilt, responsibility, and fear of losing unit cohesion.
- Later cooperation should keep the sense that both are learning how to carry the dead.

## Will and Daryl

- Daryl is Will's commanding officer and a rough caretaker.
- Will can be embarrassed or exasperated by Daryl's teasing, but respects her command ability.
- Daryl's orders can be crude and warm in the same breath.

## Daryl and Lilia

- Daryl treats Lilia as a new but wounded Marine who must be folded into the unit.
- She often cuts through stiffness and forces emotional/operational adjustment.
- `Big Mom` describes this family-like command style.

## Lilia and Melvina

- Melvina often provides perspective and softens Lilia's rigid duty mindset.
- Their exchanges may be playful, explanatory, or gently challenging.

## Command and Ceremony

- Allston's speeches should be stately and sober.
- Funeral/memorial language should preserve solemnity.
- Do not flatten ceremony into everyday conversation.

## Battle Tone

- Battle lines are short, urgent, and often clipped.
- Keep callsigns and unit roles clear.
- Profanity can remain when JP is profane. Do not sanitize unless the user decides.
